General

mycollegehomepage.com or mchp, is changing the way students become organized and maintain it while in college. We're a convenient homepage application that takes your entire life, and puts it all on one page.

If you Set us as your Homepage (that's the point). You're already one-step closer to becoming organized.

How do I choose my college?

When you provide your email address during the sign up process, we determine what college you are a student at, and then "enroll" you in that college on mchp. If you transfer schools, all you need to do is go to your account page and select the Transfer Schools button.

How do I add my classes?

After signing up with mchp, you'll be directed to your Schedule page to add your classes to your schedule. Start by searching for a course code and number, ex:ECON 200, and add them by clicking on the green "Join" button. Each time you add a class to your schedule, you'll see it on the left of the page under the section titled, "Currently Enrolled". If you are unable to find a certain class, you can always Create a Class which takes no more than 15 seconds. Remember to provide the correct information when creating a class so your classmates can also find it via search. To report an incorrect class or a duplicate, please notify us.

How do I set mchp as my homepage?

We created mchp with the intent that students would find it beneficial to have us set as their browser homepage. You can do this by clicking on Tools Icon in the top right of the navigation bar, or you can Set us as your Homepage right here.

Homepage

Your Homepage features your Quicklinks, Today Section, College Pulse, Classmates, and the News. It caters to you and your needs, in a convenient and simple manner.

Quicklinks

Quicklinks are clickable links that provide quick access to your University's most commonly used on-line academic websites. For example, your University's email system is a quicklink. Quicklinks are meant to be easy to find and always at your finger tips, since you will be using them on a daily basis.

Today

The Today Section serves one purpose, and that's to inform you of what you have to do "Today". Every day brings new obstacles, and mchp is here to help you stay on top, no matter how much you have going on.

Pulse

The Pulse is a feed that displays all activity relevant to you. You'll see events various activity items, such as when new users join your class, when new assignments are added to a calendar you follow, and when new documents are added to any of your classes.

News

The News displays stories from around the web, based on your interests. To change your interests, simply click the at the top right of the News section. Remember, all the interests in blue will be shown in the news section.

Classes

Your Classes page manages all of your classes on a single page, while remaining clutter-free. Each one of your classes has neatly outlined sections for Class Activity, Classmates, and Documents.

Activity

Every class has stuff going on in it, right? For example, when someone new joins the class, or a new document is added, or when an assignment is edited on the calendar. All of these things are neatly displayed in the respective Class's Activity section, chronologically, so you can stay up to date with what's going on in your class without stressing about it.

Classmates

Don't be a stranger! It's nice to be able to see who else is in your class with you. Every class has a list of the students who are currently enrolled in it along with yourself. These are your classmates, so be nice to them. You never know when you'll need help from them .

Documents

Every class has a list of documents that have been uploaded by your fellow classmates or other students on mchp. These documents should help you prepare for exams, quizzes, projects and more! If you want to view a document, just click on its thumbnail or title.

If you are looking to Upload and Sell a Study Guide, simply choose the class it's associated with, provide a title, description, and a price, and that's it. Calendar

Your Calendar is what you make it. Add assignment dates and class events, gain classmates as followers, and stay organized. You can keep your calendar private, too. The choice is yours!

Creating a Calendar

You can always Create a Calendar by clicking the Options located on the Calendar page. There are two types of calendars you can create:

  • Public: this calendar is for assignment dates and events for a specific class. Your classmates can follow this calendar. When you change something on it, they'll be notified.
  • Private: this is just like a public calendar, but it's private. Only you will be able to see the events you add to it.

Class Meetings, End Date, and Calendar Description

You should triple check that the meeting days and times of the class you are creating a calendar for are correct. If it's public, this is how your classmates will identify the calendar to follow, and they aren't going to follow a calendar that doesn't have the correct meeting days and times, and end date. If you are creating a class calendar but keeping it private, it is also essential to make sure this information is correct, as many of the calendar features are dependent on this information.

Adding Events and Assignment Dates

At the beginning of each semester, there WILL be an opportunity for you to add each of your class's assignment dates from your class's syllabus or schedule of assignments (if your professor provides these), into a calendar you've created on mchp. We offer an incredibly simple and Easy to Use Template so you can add multiple assignment dates at once, and then add them all to one of your class calendars with just a single click. This is to take away the hassle of navigating day to day on the calendar and adding only a single event at a time. You can still create single events from the main Calendar page by clicking on any calendar day.

What type of events should I add to my calendar?

You should strive to make it better than what your professor would do if he or she were to manage a calendar on their own! This way, your classmates are happy to follow your calendar, your calendar will get better ratings. Assignments typically include what is "due" in a class or what is required of students. The best way to determine what you should include when adding your class's assignments and events is to ask yourself, "Would I personally want to be aware of this?" If the answer is "Yes", then you should definitely add it to the class calendar you manage!

Points

Transactions on mchp are easy and secured, using Points. Cash out your funds straight to your debit card.

How do I purchase Points?

The currency used on mchp is Points. Points can be purchased with a valid credit or debit card. You will only need to enter your card info once, and mchp will remember it for you for your future purchases.You can also purchase points by using your Earnings. It is not possible to cash-out points.

All transactions within mchp are secured and verified, and are protected with up to 256-bit Secure Sockets Layer encryption.

How do I earn Points?

You can earn points by referring your friends to sign up on mchp, and the points you earn can be spent on purchasing anything you'd like on mchp. You can also upload syllabi and earn points. When you joined mchp, we created a unique referral code (yours is: {{referral_code}}) and link (yours is: {{request.get_host}}/?ref={{referral_link}}) that will keep track of how many people sign up on mchp via one of the methods below. Your referral code and link can always be accessed from your Account Page. Here are the ways you can earn referral points:

  • When someone clicks on your referral link ({{request.get_host}}/?ref={{referral_link}}) and signs up.
  • When someone signs up and enters your referral code in the Redeem Referral box.
  • When someone clicks on a link you've shared and signs up. Such as a facebook link or when you copy a document or calendar link and paste it for others to access.

What are Earnings?

Your Earnings are represented as actual dollar amounts. Every time you sell a document on mchp, your earnings will increase. Earnings are not the same as points, however they can be used to purchase points. You can cash out your earnings if you have at least $25 worth of earnings in your piggy bank. To cash out your earnings, all you need to do is provide a valid debit card.
